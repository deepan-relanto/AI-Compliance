AI as a practical workplace capability06 / 19
Introduction

Six dangerous myths create risk in AI-enabled workplaces.

"AI thinks like a human."

AI recognizes patterns in data. It has no understanding, intentions, or awareness in the human sense.

"AI is always right - it is a computer."

AI can produce fluent, confident, and completely incorrect answers. Output always needs human judgement.

"AI will replace all our jobs."

AI changes tasks more than it eliminates roles. Humans remain accountable for all decisions.

"AI learns from everything I type."

Most assistants do not learn in real time. Data handling depends on the tool and settings - use company-approved tools.

"AI and automation are the same."

Automation follows fixed rules. AI handles variation and ambiguity. They are different and complementary.

"You must be technical to use AI."

Clear thinking and clear instructions matter far more than technical skill.

Key Concepts 3.7-3.8

Hallucinations are not bugs - they are a natural consequence of how AI generates text.

3.7 Training Data

AI learned from vast text such as websites, books, and articles up to a cutoff date. Post-cutoff events and internal data are invisible unless provided in conversation.

3.8 Hallucinations

AI can produce fluent, confident, and completely fabricated information: invented statistics, non-existent citations, and wrong dates. This is a core behavior.

When are hallucinations most likely?

Very High

Specific internal data: policies, org charts, contracts

High

Named entities: executives, citation details, statistics

Medium

Events after training cutoff date

Low

Well-documented general knowledge

Critical habit: verify every AI-provided fact, figure, citation, or name before using it in client-facing or decision-making material.

Key Concepts 3.9-3.11

Prompt quality and context are the levers employees actually control.

3.9 Tokens

tokens ~= 75,000 words

Weak Prompt

"Write an email about the delay."

Missing audience, tone, length, purpose, and constraints.

Strong Prompt

"Draft a short apologetic email to a client for a one-week delay. Professional tone, under 150 words, revised date 24 June."

Includes task, audience, format, and constraints.

Context

Everything the AI can see: prompts, answers, and documents you provide.

Visibility Limit

AI has no knowledge of your team or projects unless you provide it.

Grounding Habit

Paste the policy section before asking a policy question.

Grounded context equals grounded answers.

What employees need to know

Key Concepts 3.14

Governance controls are required to mitigate AI reliability and compliance risks.

High

Confidently Wrong

Fluency does not equal accuracy. AI generates plausible text, not verified facts.

High

Knowledge Cutoffs

Post-cutoff events and internal data are invisible unless provided in context.

Medium

Reflects Bias

Training data can reproduce stereotyped or skewed outputs, especially in HR and compliance contexts.

High

No True Understanding

AI predicts plausible next tokens. It does not reason, verify facts, or grasp consequences like humans.

Medium

Arithmetic Can Fail

For exact calculations, use a spreadsheet. Have AI explain logic, not compute critical numbers.

Critical

Not Accountable

Responsibility for AI output remains with you and your organization - not the tool.

Key Takeaways

Six principles govern safe and effective AI use in the enterprise.

01

Fluency ≠ Accuracy

AI sounds confident even when wrong. Human verification is non-negotiable for client-facing output.

02

Context Is Everything

AI without context produces generic output. Provide specifics; get grounded answers.

03

Choose the Right Tool

Automation for predictable rule-based tasks. AI for language, variation, and ambiguity - with review.

04

Prompts Drive Quality

Clear task, defined audience, specified format, and stated constraints produce better output.

05

Know Failure Modes

Hallucinations, cutoffs, bias, and arithmetic errors are predictable and preventable with the right habits.

06

You Stay Accountable

The employee who uses AI output owns its accuracy and consequences. No tool can change that.
